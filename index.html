§ 01 The Edge
Why a journalist beats a designer at small-business websites.

Twenty years of editing. Now applied to your website.

01

Find the lead.

Most small-business websites bury the one thing that makes the business worth choosing. Finding it and moving it to the front is what editors do for a living.

02

Cut the filler.

"Empowering solutions." "Seamless experiences." "Passionate about quality." Editors delete this kind of language on sight. Customers do the same — about a quarter-second after they land.

03

Write like a person.

Your website should sound like you on a good day, not like a brochure from 2011. Every line gets rewritten so a customer recognizes a human on the other end.

04

Build it right.

Clean code. Fast load. Readable on a phone. Trust signals where they belong. Booking or contact where customers expect it. Shipped, not just designed.

§ 04 How It Works
A clear, repeatable process — not a stack of mystery deliverables.

How the work actually gets done.

Step 01

Brief

A short conversation and a written brief. What the business does, who it's for, and where the current site is working against it.

Step 02

Research

Customer voice, reviews, and competitors read closely. The real story — what the business is genuinely better at — comes out here.

Step 03

Write

Every line of copy rewritten. Every headline earned. Every section structured to move a reader from landing to action. The site is built around the writing, not the other way around.

Step 04

Ship

The site launches. A short pre-launch checklist catches anything rough. Thirty days later, a review pass sharpens whatever's still dull.
